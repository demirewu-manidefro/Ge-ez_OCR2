Ge'ez Manuscript OCR System

A Deep Learning Approach for Handwritten Ethiopic Script Recognition

About the Project

Ge'ez is one of the world's oldest scripts, originating from the Aksumite civilization and serving as the liturgical language of the Ethiopian and Eritrean Orthodox Churches. Millions of handwritten manuscripts are preserved in monasteries across Lalibela and Lake Tana — yet these culturally and historically invaluable documents are rapidly deteriorating.

This project builds a practical deep learning OCR system that converts scanned Ge'ez manuscript images into editable digital text, making these irreplaceable documents searchable, preservable, and accessible to scholars, churches, and communities worldwide.

Why It's Challenging

Recognizing handwritten Ge'ez is uniquely difficult due to the characteristics of the Ethiopic Abugida script:

  • Over 238 characters with high visual similarity
  • Up to 7 vowel forms per consonant
  • Significant variation in handwriting styles across different scribes and historical periods
  • Severe degradation, noise, and geometric distortions in ancient manuscripts
  • No existing practical OCR system designed specifically for handwritten Ge'ez

Our Solution — Hybrid STN-ResNet-CRNN

We designed and trained a hybrid deep learning architecture that combines four components working together in a pipeline:

  • STN (Spatial Transformer Network) — Corrects geometric distortions such as rotation, skew, and perspective that are common in ancient handwritten manuscripts.
  • ResNet (Residual Network Backbone) — Extracts deep and discriminative visual features from manuscript images while preserving fine character details.
  • Bi-LSTM (Bidirectional LSTM) — Captures sequential dependencies in text by learning context from both forward and backward directions simultaneously.
  • CTC (Connectionist Temporal Classification) — Enables end-to-end training and decoding without requiring explicit character-level segmentation.

Key Takeaways

  • The Hybrid STN-ResNet-CRNN outperforms TrOCR in every metric
  • It is 27x smaller and 2x faster to train than TrOCR
  • Data quality matters more than data quantity — the clean 9K dataset beat the noisy 16K dataset
  • The STN component effectively handles geometric distortions unique to ancient manuscripts

Dataset

Primary data was collected from Debre Birhan Debre Tesbha Kidus Giyorgis Monastery.

  • 157 high-resolution manuscript pages scanned
  • 16,036 initial line-level image-label pairs generated
  • 9,036 high-quality images selected after careful filtering for noise and labeling accuracy
  • Preprocessing included CLAHE enhancement, geometric normalization, Unicode standardization, and data augmentation
